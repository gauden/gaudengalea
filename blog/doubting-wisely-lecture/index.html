Doubting Wisely – Slide Guide

01. WHO European Public Health Leadership Course

Summary: The cover slide for the sixth edition of the WHO/Europe Public Health Leadership Course, organised by the Quality of Care Centre, Athens, Greece with the in-person element organised on 10-14th November 2025.

02. Doubting Wisely

Summary: Values, humility, and effectiveness in public health leadership. Cover slide for this presentation.

This document outlines the content of the presentation, which may be downloaded in PowerPoint format. This presentation is © Gauden Galea 2025. Licensed under CC BY 4.0 (the Creative Commons Attribution Licence).

03. The "Heroic" Leader

Summary: An AI-generated image caricaturing the idealised vision of the leader; a leader with superpowers, singular vision, and an ideal team that follows.

04. Knowledge or Certainty

Summary: Extract from the BBC documentary by Jacob Bronowski (1973), "The Ascent of Man". In this episode he shows the extreme impact that arrogance and dogma can take. References & further reading:

  • https://youtu.be/ltjI3BXKBgY?si=k-PzmFHKRR21xcQ7

05. Conflict Index – context for leadership

Summary: Political violence increased; many countries at extreme or high conflict levels. References & further reading:

  • Source: ACLED (Armed Conflict Location & Event Data) Conflict Index Report (Dec 2024)
  • https://acleddata.com/series/acled-conflict-index

06. Leadership in Disruptive times

Summary: A table compares and contrasts the concepts of uncertainty and doubt from the point of view of the leader.

07. Exercise: doubt vs uncertainty on air

Summary: Provocative prompts to distinguish doubt from uncertainty in practice -- in a hypothetical interview with a journalist.

08. Commercial determinants of Health

Summary: Introduces the weaponisation of doubt by vested interests in the commercial sector, weaponising doubt through dark money, distortion of evidence, lobbying, and targeting of the vulnerable, among others. References & further reading:

  • Michaels D. The Triumph of Doubt: Dark Money and the Science of Deception. Oxford University Press, 2020. https://global.oup.com/academic/product/the-triumph-of-doubt-9780190922665
  • Maani N, Petticrew M, Galea S (eds.). The Commercial Determinants of Health. Oxford University Press, 2023. https://global.oup.com/academic/product/the-commercial-determinants-of-health-9780197578759
  • Oreskes N, Conway EM. Merchants of Doubt: How a Handful of Scientists Obscured the Truth on Issues from Tobacco Smoke to Global Warming. Bloomsbury Press, 2010. https://www.bloomsbury.com/us/merchants-of-doubt-9781608193943

09. Commercial Determinants of NCDs in Europe

Summary: A WHO/Europe publication revealing the impact of four industries: tobacco, alcohol, unhealthy foods, and fossil fuels. References & further reading:

  • World Health Organization. Regional Office for Europe (2024). Commercial Determinants of Noncommunicable Diseases in the WHO European Region. World Health Organization. Regional Office for Europe. https://iris.who.int/handle/10665/376957.

10. The Rise of Mis/Dis-information and Our Response

Summary: How false claims spread, persist, and endanger health, starting from the original (retracted) paper that suggested a link between autism and vaccination, and what evidence-based science recommends to counter them. References & further reading:

  • RETRACTED Wakefield AJ, Murch SH, Anthony A, Linnell J, al e. Ileal-lymphoid-nodular hyperplasia, non-specific colitis, and pervasive developmental disorder in children. The Lancet. 1998;351(9103):637-41.  https://ejournals.um.edu.mt/login?url=https://www.proquest.com/scholarly-journals/ileal-lymphoid-nodular-hyperplasia-non-specific/docview/199060156/se-2. doi: https://doi.org/10.1016/S0140-6736(97)11096-011096-0).
  • Broda, E., & Strömbäck, J. (2024). Misinformation, disinformation, and fake news: lessons from an interdisciplinary, systematic literature review. Annals of the International Communication Association, 48(2), 139–166. https://doi.org/10.1080/23808985.2024.2323736
  • van der Linden, S., Albarracín, D., Fazio, L., Freelon, D., Roozenbeek, J., Swire-Thompson, B., & Van Bavel, J. (2025). Using psychological science to understand and fight health misinformation: An APA consensus statement.American Psychologist. Advance online publication. https://doi.org/10.1037/amp0001598 

11. Summary APA Recommendations

Summary: The eight recommendations for responding to mis/dis-information made by the Oct 2025 APA consensus statement. References & further reading:

  • van der Linden, S., Albarracín, D., Fazio, L., Freelon, D., Roozenbeek, J., Swire-Thompson, B., & Van Bavel, J. (2025). Using psychological science to understand and fight health misinformation: An APA consensus statement.American Psychologist. Advance online publication. https://doi.org/10.1037/amp0001598 

12. Know your values

Summary: Clarify values to guide decisions under uncertainty. This is a prompt slide for participants to reflect on what their own values, what motivates their own leadership in public health.

13. Regulatory frame for data, AI, and devices (EU)

Summary: An example of encoding values in legislation using the EU digital innovation space and how it encodes four key sets of values: p\*rivacy, community, innovation, devices, and AI risk management.* References & further reading:

  • GDPR (2016) Link
  • European Health Data Space (EUHDS, 2025) Link
  • A Briefing on the EU AI Act (2024) Link698792_EN.pdf)
  • EU Medical Devices Regulation (2017) Link

14 and 15. “On Bullshit” — An essay by Harry Frankfurt

Summary: Contrasting "bullshit" with doubt and uncertainty; extracting from the classic paper by Harry Frankfurt the definition, means of recognition, and means of avoidance of bullshit, in oneself as much as in others. References & further reading:

  • Frankfurt H. On Bullshit. Raritan Quarterly Review. Volume 6, Number 2, Fall 1986) https://raritanquarterly.rutgers.edu/issue-index/all-articles/attachment/560/333

16. Know your tools

Summary: Introduces a section where the pros and cons of two tools are discussed, one a mechanical, data science tool, and the other a higher level, conceptual tool defining a mindset in public health leadership.

17. Excel pitfalls for data science

Summary: A participatory exercise in which the audience participates in identifying the shortcomings of a spreadsheet in data analysis. 

18. Famous Excel errors

Summary: Three real-world spreadsheet failures that shaped public health, finance, and governance. References & further reading:

  • Hern A. Covid: how Excel may have caused loss of 16,000 test results in England. The Guardian. 6 October 2020. https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2020/oct/05/how-excel-may-have-caused-loss-of-16000-covid-tests-in-england 
  • Croll GJ. The Reification of an Incorrect and Inappropriate Spreadsheet Model. Proceedings of the EuSpRIG 2017 Conference “Spreadsheet Risk Management.” https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.1801.10249
  • Reinhart CM, Rogoff KS. Growth in a Time of Debt. NBER Working Paper No. 15639, January 2010. https://www.nber.org/papers/w15639
  • Herndon T, Ash M, Pollin R. Does high public debt consistently stifle economic growth? A critique of Reinhart and Rogoff. Cambridge Journal of Economics 2014; 38: 257–79. https://content.csbs.utah.edu/\~mli/Economics%207004/Does\_High\_Public\_Debt\_Consistently\_Stifle\_Economic\_Growth\_\_CJE\_version.pdf

Summary: Notebook‑driven, executable documents for auditable results -- an antidote to avoid or mitigate typicalspreadsheet errors. References & further reading:

20. Precautionary Principle — definition

Summary: Act to prevent serious harm despite scientific uncertainty when cost‑effective measures exist. References & further reading:

  • Rio Declaration on Environment and Development (1992) https://www.un.org/en/development/desa/population/migration/generalassembly/docs/globalcompact/A\_CONF.151\_26\_Vol.I\_Declaration.pdf

21. Precautionary Principle — operationalisation

Summary: Trigger, proportional actions, integration with risk assessment/management/communication.

Summary: From moratoria to global treaties as pre‑emptive safeguards. References & further reading:

24. Sir Ronald Fisher's Crusade

Summary: The great statistician, Sir Ronald Aylmer Fisher, had his own biases, and insisted that the link between cancer and smoking was a case of "correlation, not causation". References & further reading:

  • Fisher, R. Cancer and Smoking. Nature 182, 596 (1958). https://doi.org/10.1038/182596a0
  • Fisher, R. and Yates, F. Statistical tables for biological, agricultural and medical research, (6th Edition).  https://digital.library.adelaide.edu.au/server/api/core/bitstreams/21f22d02-4a39-4650-8f09-71434ad02897/content

25. Thinking Fast and Slow

Summary: How cognitive shortcuts shape judgment and how overconfidence in ‘System 1’ thinking led to flawed research on priming and bias; the theory on fast and slow thinking is still very relevant but still needs us to evaluate the research behind it. References & further reading:

  • Kahneman D. Thinking, Fast and Slow. 2011. https://us.macmillan.com/books/9780374533557/thinkingfastandslow
  • Schimmack U. Reconstruction of a Train Wreck: How Priming Research Went off the Rails. Replicability‑Index Blog, 2 February 2017. https://replicationindex.com/2017/02/02/reconstruction-of-a-train-wreck-how-priming-research-went-of-the-rails/
